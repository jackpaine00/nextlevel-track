College Track & Field Recruiting Info

1. Recruiting Standards

These are general ranges — each program is different, but this gives you a starting point. Most programs are going to offer within the range for the sprints and distance marks, but jumps are subject to change vastly depending on the program and their focus/if they even have a coach.

  • Men's 60m (D1): 6.6s - 7.1s
  • Womens's 60m (D1): 7.1s - 7.6s
  • Men’s 100m (D1): 10.0s – 10.8s
  • Women’s 100m (D1): 11.2s – 12.2s
  • Men’s 200m (D1): 20.0s – 22.3s
  • Womens’s 200m (D1): 22.2s – 24.5s
  • Men’s 400m (D1): 46.0s – 48.2s
  • Womens’s 400m (D1): 51.0s – 53.8s
  • Men’s 800m (D1): 1:48s – 1:54s
  • Women’s 800m (D1): 2:00s – 2:12s
  • Men’s 1600m: 4:02 – 4:22
  • Women’s 1600m: 4:22 – 5:05
  • Long Jump: Men 22’6"+, Women 17’9"+
  • High Jump: Men 6’5”+, Women 5’5”+
  • Pole Vault: Men 14’6”+, Women 11’3”+

2. Email Template for Coaches

Here’s a basic structure you can follow when contacting college coaches:

Subject: Interest in [University] Track & Field Program – Class of 20XX

Dear Coach [Last Name],

My name is [Your Name], and I’m a [grade/year] at [High School Name] in [City, State]. I’m very interested in your track and field program and wanted to introduce myself.

Personal Bests:
– 100m: [Time]
– 200m: [Time]
– GPA: [GPA] | SAT/ACT: [Score if applicable]

I’ve attached my resume and a link to my Athletic.net profile. I’d love to learn more about your program and recruiting needs, as I beleive I would be a great fit to your program. Thank you for your time!

Sincerely,  
[Your Full Name]  
[Phone Number]  
[Email Address]
